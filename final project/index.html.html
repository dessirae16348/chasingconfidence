Coping Strategies

Self esteem can be extremely difficult to take on.
It can feel for a person as if they aren't good as others and there
isn't anything that can be done to fix their problems.
Many also believe that the only person that can change the way they feel is
another person: but in reality the only peron that can change you is you.
Here is a few tips to help you feel better.

FOCUS ON YOURSELF

- Don't compare yourself to others
- Focus on things you can actually change
- Do things that make you happy
- Think of things you're good at
- Know your worth
- Learn to be your own best friend
- Focus on your own life

DON'T PUT YOURSELF

- Surround yourself with positive people
- Understand that you are in control
of your mind and self-esteem
- Be aware of what you are subjecting
yourself to, and how it makes you feel

DON'T FOCUS ON THE NEGATIVE
SIDE OF THINGS

- Don't think everything has to be perfect
- Be non-judgemental to yourself and others, just the way you are

UNDERTAND YOUR FEELINGS SO
YOU CAN COMBAT THEM

- Combat negative thoughts
- Understand the reason you are feeling
down, begin with the root of the problem
- Write down your negative, and positive
feelings down in a journal.

SUCCESS STORIES

Natasha Wessler

Natasha Wessler struggled with having bad acne all throughout most of her life. She heard people say things like “how can someone love a person with acne” or “you just need to wash your face and it will be gone.” In college she met a girl with the same name as her and when they became friends she told her that she had been referring to her as “the Ugly Natasha.” Hearing these things obviously had a negative impact on her self esteem. One day Natasha came across an instagram page of a girl who posted confidently with her acne. Seeing that page inspired her to not feel so ashamed of her acne. She decided she wanted to be happy and not worry so much about her acne, and even though it was hard, she did it. https://medium.com/@natashawessler/another-story-about-a-girl-with-low-self-esteem-7eed65337b3e

Ashley Graham

Ashley Graham is a American plus size model who celebrates body positivity and self love. She wasn’t always very confident in her body; she began modeling at age twelve and moved to New York at seventeen. She struggled not fitting the average model body, and at one point even was thinking of giving up, claiming “There was a moment when I was 18 years old and I said: ‘I can’t do this anymore’,”. But she persevered, gaining national attention and leading the way for other curvy women alike while spreading body positivity. She did this by creating a mantra for herself “You are fine, you are brilliant, and you are beautiful and bold.”. This was difficult to do. Everyone was telling her how she needs to change, even her own father. But she learned to believe in herself and gain self love. https://www.nbcnews.com/know-your-value/feature/how-model-ashley-graham-finds-confidence-courage-ncna1002176

Alexa Wilson

Alexa Wison dealt with low self esteem, ever since middle school. Before she didn’t understand why, she didn’t even realise it was low self esteem. She found value and self-worth from external sources. And this worked for her until she was alone, which lead her to surround herself with people continuously. It wasn’t until she was twenty-three years old contemplating graduate school. But she was afraid of going, afraid of not being smart enough, of it being too hard and failing. And it clicked in her she realized that in her life she passed on many opportunities because of her self doubt saying “Somehow, that [signing up for graduate school] got me thinking about all the things I didn’t do in life because I was afraid. Because I didn’t think I was smart enough, or pretty enough, or talented enough, or good enough, or whatever.”. She realized that all of lacking self confidence came from elementary school. Learning the source of her problems allowed her to face them and re-teach herself how to be confident. She created a mantra of her own “I started telling myself that I was strong, confident, capable, and beautiful. I would say this to myself each night before I went to sleep.”. This created a cycle of her loving and believing in herself more than ever. https://www.youshareproject.com/overcame-low-self-confidence-changed-life/

About Us

We are a group of girls from Girls Who Code ages 15-18. Our mission is to spread awareness and offer solutions to dealing with low self esteem. Everyone has dealt with negative feelings about themselves at some point in their lives, and we are here to make people’s days a little bit brighter.
